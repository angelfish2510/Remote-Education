The badge requirements:

  1. Get Transportation Smart - Pick ONE of these activities.
    • Get a bike ready to ride - put air in the tire, check the brakes, oil, etc. If you take a bike to get maintenance, you could ask the technician to talk through the maintenance with your daughter.
    • Help take care of a car - (We will do this one) Learn about the lights and gauges on the dashboard, check the oil, windshield washing fluid. Have a parent step on the brake - check the brake lights, turn signals and headlights.
    • Use public transportation (But not right now!!) Learn how to use the trimet website to map a trip and take it.
  2. Make your clothes look great. - Pick ONE of these activities
    • Become a laundry expert. Learn about separating laundry and different types of fabrics that require different care. Learn to remove a stain. Be your family's helper and do three or more loads for practice.
    • Learn two basic sewing skills - Learn to thread a needle, do a basic stitch, a whip stitch and sew on a button. You can take a couple of pieces of fabric squares - do one stitch along one side, the other on the other side and sew a button in the middle. Whip stitch could also be used to sew a badge on a girl's uniform.
    • Be a clothes organization guru.
    • Watch an episode of Marie Kando and then organize your closet! Ok - the episode wasn't in the Girl Scout book, but the girls might learn from it. Organize your closet or dresser. You might sort by color or length, make a shoe rack, use labeled clear plastic boxes or shoe boxes to hold different items or accessories. Parent note: Learn how to say thank you and give stuff away or sell it on craigslist or facebook marketplace to earn money!
  3. Break a bad habit - See link below for a good framework. - Just pick ONE habit and work on it over the next few weeks. Commit it to writing at a minimum and talk about it frequently.
    • At school - putting homework off to the last minute, forgetting to study for a quiz, forgetting schoolwork at home.
    • At home - forgetting to brush teeth, not putting away your clothes, arguing with siblings.
    • Be a clothes organization guru.
    • With your friends - being bossy with your friends, talking too much about yourself, interrupting.

    If they do this, it'd be great if they can share what they did at a meeting. (You could turn this into a writing exercise - How To break... or Why I want to... or similar)

  4. Help around the house.
    • Solve a pesky plumbing problem. Sink plugged? Have your daughter help you disassemble and clean the U trap. Test a smoke alarm and replace batteries. Hang something up. Figure out which type of fastener is best for the wall. Measure, etc.

    Pick something that fulfills this requirement - these are just examples. I think assembling furniture, cleaning the fridge, all counts!

  5. Show off your independence - Pick ONE
    • Stay home alone for a few hours.
    • Run a family errand - Make a grocery list, do the shopping while you are somewhere nearby.
    • -Go out with a friend - go to a movie or eat a meal. Sit at your own table and pay the bill. (I let my kids order their own milkshakes once and they came back with a large!)

    Option 1 sounds like a good one right now!
